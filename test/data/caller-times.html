Remembering the magic that made Frampton come alive

Frampton celebrating epic album's 35 years

  • By Alan Sculley/Special to the Caller-Times
  • Corpus Christi Caller Times
  • Posted July 8, 2011 at 4:37 a.m.
  • Email
  • Discuss
  • Share »

CORPUS CHRISTI — Peter Frampton is celebrating the 35th anniversary of his blockbuster live album, “Frampton Comes Alive,” with a summer/fall tour that finds him playing the entire CD in sequence for the first time since 1976.

Of course, Frampton never expected “Frampton Comes Alive” to make him rock’s biggest act during 1976 and 1977. But he sensed it might turn him into a legitimate headlining artist.

The reason: He’d seen a similar scenario with his previous band, Humble Pie. That band had slowly grown its audience, hitting a new high with the studio album “Rock On.” And when Humble Pie followed that record with the two-album live set “Performance Rocking The Fillmore” (Frampton’s last album with the group), it went gold and took the band to new heights.

“I felt the ‘Frampton’ album was my ‘Rock On,’” Frampton told “Weekly Chatter" in a recent phone interview, mentioning his self-titled fourth studio album. “I was following exactly the same (trajectory).”

Frampton still can’t say for sure why “Frampton Comes Alive” caught on to such a phenomenal degree. But he thinks radio play for “Baby, I Love Your Way” and “Show Me The Way” (from the “Frampton” album) helped. But the bigger factor, he said, might have been the performance itself.

“Musically and audience wise it was a magical night,” Frampton said. “There was an energy that came from both the band and the audience that created something that come through the speakers when you hear it. You cannot help but smile.”

Quick Hits

-- Björk is going multimedia with her latest project, “Biophilia.” In addition to a 10-track CD (to be released later this year), the project includes 10 apps (one for each song), a documentary film, a special set of live shows and an animated interactive website.

- The late soul great Sam Cooke has had a section of the street on which he grew up — 36th Street in Chicago — named Same Cooke Way in his honor.

- “Wood and Doors,” the new CD by Cliff Eberhardt and James Lee Stanley (in stores July 12) features acoustic versions of 12 Doors songs, plus something more — guest appearances from Doors members John Densmore and Robbie Krieger.

- Red Baraat will release a live CD, “Bootleg Bhangra,” on July 12.

- Reverse The Curse will release its debut CD, “Hither & Yon,” on July 12.

- George Lynch (former guitarist with Dokken and frontman of his bands, Lynch Mob and Souls Of We) will release a solo CD, “Kill All Control,” on July 19. Lynch Mob will tour from July 29 through Aug. 27.

- Other acts releasing CDs on July 19 include: City Of Ships (“Minor World”); Pursesnatchers (“A Pattern Language”); the Roadside Graves (“We Can Take Care Of Ourselves”); Killinger (“Killinger”); Brilliant Colors (“Again and Again”); Pestilence (“Doctrine”); Richard Youngs (“Amplifying Host”); Tomorrows Tulips(“Eternally Teenage”); and Golden State (“Division”).

- Boston Spaceships (featuring Robert Pollard of Guided By Voices) will release its debut CD, “Let It Beard,” on Aug. 2.

- Moreland & Arbuckle will release its second CD, “Just A Dream,” on Aug. 23.

- The Spin Doctors will release a two-disc 20th anniversary edition of its breakthrough album, “Pocket Full of Kryptonite,” on Aug. 30.

- Actor Hugh Laurie (of “House”) will release his debut CD, “Let Them Talk,” on Sept. 6. The CD was produced by Joe Henry and includes contributions from Irma Thomas, Tom Jones and Allen Toussaint.

- The Horrible Crowes (featuring Brian Fallon of the Gaslight Anthem) will release its debut CD, “Elsie,” on Sept. 6.

- Nick Lowe will release his first CD in four years, “The Old Magic,” on Sept. 13. Lowe also will open for Wilco from Sept. 13 through Oct. 5.

- Pixies frontman Black Francis and Reid Paley have collaborated on the CD, “Paley & Francis,” which will be released on Sept. 13.

- Mates Of State will release its new CD, “Mountaintops,” on Sept. 13.

- Opeth will release a new CD, “Heritage,” on Sept. 20.

- Matt Maher will release his latest CD, “The Love In Between,” on Sept. 20.

- Megafun’s second CD, a self-titled effort, will be released on Sept. 20.

- Eagles Of Death Metal frontman Jesse Hughes will release his first solo CD (under the name Boots Electric) on Sept. 20. It’s called “Honkey Kong.”

Road Trips

- The Disappears will tour the U.S. and Canada from July 20 through Sept. 9.

- Lazarus A.D. will headline the No Prisoners, No Mercy tour, from July 22 through Aug. 6.

- The reunited Big Audio Dynamite (featuring Mick Jones of the Clash) has U.S. dates from Aug. 2-12.

- Keb Mo will tour from Aug. 4 through Oct. 8.

- The band Basement will tour the states from Aug. 9-28.

- Richard Buckner and David Kilgour will co-headline a tour from Aug. 16-28.

- With the final nine dates (mostly on the west coast) now in place, the Carnival of Madness tour is set to run from Aug. 26, through Oct. 14.

- The reformed Yardbirds will tour the states from Sept. 1-24.

- Electronic artist Chad Valley will tour the states from Sept. 12-28.

- UFO will tour the west coast and Southwest from Sept. 15 through Oct. 8.
