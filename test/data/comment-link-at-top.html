Kings talk to many guards, including UConn's Walker

Share
By Jason Jones
jejones@sacbee.com
Published: Saturday, May. 21, 2011 - 12:00 am | Page 1C
Last Modified: Sunday, May. 22, 2011 - 2:48 pm

CHICAGO Kemba Walker has heard about the Kings' need for a point guard entering the 2011 NBA draft.

"I hear they want a point guard so they can move 'Reke off the ball," Walker said of Tyreke Evans. "That's something that would be pretty cool. I know 'Reke. We grew up together playing each other in the all-star games. That would be a cool experience."

Whether the Kings follow that plan remains to be seen. Walker, who led Connecticut to the 2010-11 NCAA championship, was among the prospects the Kings interviewed Friday at the NBA draft combine, and one of the many guards the Kings have spoken with this week.

Despite the talk about finding a pure point guard, the Kings have shown an interest in Walker, a point guard with a scoring mentality, and a few shooting guards.

Many prospects said the Kings were interested in their ability to play either guard spot.

As last season progressed, Kings coach Paul Westphal didn't label any of his guards as "point" guards, saying the offense had two lead guards and either was capable of initiating the offense.

At season's end, Evans and Marcus Thornton were the starting guards. The Kings' best playmaker, Beno Udrih, became a reserve. When he wasn't on the floor, the team occasionally struggled getting into an offensive rhythm.

That's why many have said the Kings would use a high draft pick on a point guard.

Backup Pooh Jeter has a team option on his contract for next season. Assuming the Kings re-sign Thornton, the guard rotation with the current roster would again be Evans, Thornton and Udrih.

Even if the Kings pass on a point guard with the seventh overall pick, there will be plenty of opportunities to fill the position.

"(This draft) was point-guard shallow for a while, and then all these surprise point guards were coming out and are looking really, really strong in terms of numbers," said Ryan Blake, the NBA's co-scouting director. "I think that's going to be the most difficult choice, too."

The consensus top point guard is Duke freshman Kyrie Irving, projected to be the first pick in the draft. Kentucky freshman Brandon Knight, considered more of a floor general than a scorer, is expected to be taken quickly.

Walker probably will be available for the Kings in the first round, along with BYU's Jimmer Fredette. Players such as Cleveland State's Norris Cole and Duke's Nolan Smith could be options in the second round.

Walker is the second-shortest player (5-foot-11 without shoes) at the combine. Fredette's athleticism has been questioned. Both were big scorers in college.

Fredette said he would be able to play in an offense with two guards who share playmaking responsibilities.

"I've played on the ball and off the ball," Fredette said. "I feel more comfortable with the ball in my hands and making plays for people. I can play that way (off the ball) as long as everything goes well."

Walker said he'd have no problem not being the primary scoring threat. Some wonder if he is an undersized shooting guard.

"I'm a point guard," Walker said. "This is the first year I've had to score points for my team. Before that, I was passing and finding guys, so it won't be a tough adjustment at all."

Blake said Walker's desire to prove critics wrong could fuel his career.

"What I like about him is his ability to run the pick and roll and turn the corner and get inside," Blake said. "His ability to score is a plus because you draw defenders."
